Our Services

We provide comprehensive support to address immediate needs while building long-term community resilience.

Current Programs

Weekly Meal Distribution

Every Thursday, 5:00 PM - 7:00 PM

Hot, nutritious meals served with dignity in a welcoming community space. No questions asked, everyone is welcome. We serve approximately 200 meals each Thursday.

Monthly Clothing Donations

First Saturday of every month

Clean, quality clothing for adults and children. Special focus on work-appropriate attire, school uniforms, and seasonal items like warm coats in winter.

Skills Development Workshops

Second Saturday of every month

Basic job preparation including CV writing, interview skills, and computer literacy. We also provide guidance on accessing government services and job opportunities.

Upcoming Events

Back-to-School Drive - January 2025

Providing school supplies, uniforms, and shoes for children from low-income families. Registration opens in December.

Winter Warmth Campaign - June 2025

Distributing blankets, warm clothing, and hot soup during the coldest months of the year.

Community Garden Project - September 2025

Teaching sustainable food production and providing fresh vegetables for our meal programs.

How We Work

Our approach is built on respect, dignity, and community partnership. We work closely with local churches, schools, and businesses to maximize our impact and avoid duplication of services.

Distribution Locations

We operate from two key locations in Port Elizabeth to ensure we can reach communities most in need:

  • Central Community Hall - Serving the city center and surrounding areas
  • Township Community Center - Focused on outlying residential communities

Who We Serve

Our services are open to anyone in need, including:

  • Homeless individuals and families
  • Low-income households struggling with food security
  • Unemployed individuals seeking skills development
  • Children and elderly community members
  • Anyone facing temporary hardship
